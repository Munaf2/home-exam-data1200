Smartphones

European Accessibility Act

Norwegian government chooses to implement the European Accessibility Act into Norwegian law and is prepared to issue large fines for digital services, or devices that do not comply and adapt to the basic principles of universal design. To what degree smartphones fulfills the principles of universal design European accessibility act.

Key terms

Universal design is based on creating products that ensure accessibility and usability for all people no matter their situation or state.

Participatory processes is method that ensure that all people involved in a decision has a say in it so that everyone is represented when deciding how to develop something.

Simple and Intuitive Use: The design should be easy to understand, regardless of the user's experience, knowledge, language skills, or current concentration level.

The current usage of smartphones in everyday life

Smartphones are widely used in the world and almost everyone has one. Most people rely on their smartphones to communicate with others and really depend on them to get through everyday life. The smartphone is used to pay bills, access personal files and some people even rely on it to do their jobs. Other usage of smartphones in everyday life is social media, news, navigation, and entertainment such as games and movies. For more information visit

Three ways this technology fails to comply with key principles in universal design

The first problem with this technology is that it has a complicated and advanced design, especially for elder people. It could be difficult to navigate through the phone and even harder to adjust and personal customizations that would make it easier to use the smartphone. Also, there are a lot of functions and buttons that change settings, modes etc. and it is ten hard to keep track of them and even harder noticing if you accidentally pressed one. Read this research paper for more information ResearchGate.

Three ways this technology complies with key principles in universal design

Smartphones have many great built-in features that make it easier for people to use them such as screen readers, size adjustments and voice commands. This makes the technology more user friendly and more adaptable for everyone to use. For more information visit universaldesign.

What is the potential impact of a strict enforcement of universal design on all digital products?

First of all, more people will be able to use the products and they will be more user-friendly. This will make digital products more accessible and included to all people in society. If the product does not comply with the law, there will be economic consequences through fines. This could hurt small businesses who cannot afford to make a mistake in production and in the worst case could lead to bankruptcy. For more information visit McKinsey&Company.
